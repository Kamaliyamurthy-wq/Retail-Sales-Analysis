Retail Sales Data Analysis (SQL Project)

Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing a retail sales dataset to understand customer purchasing behavior, sales trends, and business performance. The analysis is performed using SQL to extract meaningful insights that can support data-driven decision-making.

Objectives
* Analyze overall sales performance
* Identify top-performing categories and customers
* Understand customer demographics (age, gender)
* Discover sales trends over time
* Evaluate profit and revenue patterns

Dataset Description
The dataset contains the following columns:
* transaction_id – Unique ID for each transaction
* sale_date – Date of purchase
* sale_time – Time of purchase
* customer_id – Unique customer ID
* gender – Customer gender
* age – Customer age
* category – Product category
* quantity – Number of items purchased
* price_per_unit – Price per item
* cogs – Cost of goods sold
* total_sales – Total transaction value

Data Cleaning Steps
* Checked for NULL values and handled missing categories
* Verified duplicate transaction IDs
* Converted 'sale_date' to proper DATE format

 SQL Analysis Performed
* Total revenue calculation
* Sales by category and gender
* Monthly sales trend analysis
* Top 10 customers by spending
* Age group segmentation
* Product demand analysis (quantity sold)
* Peak sales hours identification
* Profit calculation (Revenue - COGS)
* Repeat customer analysis

Key Insights
* Certain product categories contribute the highest revenue
* Customers aged '25–35' are major contributors to sales
* Sales show clear trends across months
* Peak sales occur during specific hours of the day
* A small group of customers generates a large portion of revenue
* Repeat customers play a significant role in overall sales

Tools & Technologies
* SQL (pgAdmin4)
* Data Analysis Techniques

Conclusion
This project demonstrates the practical application of SQL in analyzing retail sales data to derive meaningful business insights. By performing data cleaning, aggregation, and exploratory analysis, key trends in customer behavior, product performance, and sales patterns were identified.
The analysis highlights high-performing categories, valuable customer segments, and peak sales periods, while also incorporating profit evaluation to provide a more comprehensive view of business performance. These insights can support data-driven decision-making in areas such as marketing strategy, customer targeting, and inventory management.Overall, the project showcases strong foundational SQL skills and the ability to transform raw data into actionable insights, making it highly relevant for real-world business scenarios.
